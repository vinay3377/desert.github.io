Rajasthan Dream Tour

Jaipur, the capital city of Rajasthan, India, is a vibrant blend of history and culture, earning it the nickname "The Pink City" for its distinctive terracotta-colored buildings. Among its numerous attractions, the majestic Amer Fort stands out, offering visitors a glimpse into royal history with its intricate architecture and panoramic views. The City Palace, a splendid fusion of Mughal and Rajasthani styles, is another must-visit, housing museums that showcase royal artifacts. Hawa Mahal, or the Palace of Winds, captivates with its unique honeycomb structure and countless windows. Additionally, the Jantar Mantar observatory, a UNESCO World Heritage site, fascinates with its astronomical instruments. For a touch of spirituality, the serene Birla Mandir and the intricate Govind Dev Ji Temple provide peaceful retreats. Jaipur's vibrant bazaars, such as Johari Bazaar and Bapu Bazaar, offer a delightful shopping experience, where one can find traditional handicrafts, jewelry, and textiles. With its rich heritage and bustling markets, Jaipur offers a memorable and culturally immersive experience for all visitors.

Udaipur "City of Lakes,"

Udaipur, often referred to as the "City of Lakes," is a picturesque destination in Rajasthan, India, renowned for its shimmering lakes, magnificent palaces, and vibrant culture. The crown jewel of Udaipur is the stunning City Palace, a sprawling complex of palaces, courtyards, and museums that showcase the grandeur of Rajasthan's royal history. Just a stone's throw away is the serene Lake Pichola, where visitors can enjoy a boat ride and admire the ethereal beauty of the Lake Palace, a luxurious hotel that appears to float on the water. The Jag Mandir, another island palace on Lake Pichola, offers breathtaking views and a tranquil ambiance. For nature enthusiasts, Saheliyon Ki Bari, a historic garden adorned with fountains and marble pavilions, provides a refreshing retreat. The Monsoon Palace, perched on a hilltop, offers panoramic vistas of the city and its surrounding countryside. Udaipur's old city is a maze of narrow lanes filled with bustling markets, vibrant bazaars, and charming havelis. The city's vibrant cultural scene is reflected in its numerous art galleries, traditional music performances, and colorful festivals. With its romantic charm and rich heritage, Udaipur promises an enchanting experience for all visitors.

Jaisalmer Desert Safari

When you imagine about visit to Rajasthan the top three things comes-in the forts & palaces, Tigers, and camels at the sand dunes. Jaisalmer offers a chance to escape the big city's chaos and let you dive deep in history, folk culture and unique desert landscape to experience the way people live. If you’re planing for camel safari with overnight camping, the Khuri desert and Sam dunes (bit more touristic) are ideal desert locations not far from Jaisalmer town (approx 41-46 KM). Out in the desert you’ll be able to watch the best sunset over the vast Thar desert with a hot cup of freshly brewed chai tea. At Jaisalmer you’ll get to try adventure Jeep safari or hop on the back of one humped camel and share 1000 stories around campfire. As the sun begins to set the sky turns in multi coloured turben and the desert camp into an open air planetarium with just the crackle of the fire and the call of desert foxes the only soundtrack.
